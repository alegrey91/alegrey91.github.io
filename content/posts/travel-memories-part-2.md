I've always been a great supporter of low-budget trips.

The main reason for this is not that I don't like to spend money (this is just part of the reason 😅), but because low-budget trips are often synonymous with weird and inconvenient experiences.

These experiences, for travelers, are the best stories to tell friends once back from the trip.
The story that I'm gonna tell today embodies the essence of what I just described. This is one of my favorite stories to tell about my trips.

## The Squeaky Roommate

In the year of our Lord 2015, I was a young man intent on studying Computer Science at the University (with less than excellent grades). At that time, I was almost 24 years old and absorbed by youth interests, such as love, football, computers, and listening to music.

This last passion was like an invisible friend to me, always by my side at almost every moment of the day.
During that period, I remember listening to a myriad of music genres, consuming the most unlikely playlists available on Spotify.
?My mind was so trained that I had no problem at all in embracing new music genres, which were unknown to me at the time.
Some albums were imprinted in my memory, contrary to the database notions that I should have studied (such as [ACID](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACID) properties) for the next exam session.
The CD was still a widely used audio medium, and my car constantly reproduced the few albums I owned.
On the gold list were names like Arctic Monkeys, Radiohead, and Black Keys.

The Black Keys were, with no hesitation, my favourite band. Their sound was a perfect mix of Rock and Blues tones, and the desire to see them live was great.

The occasion came on that year, right after the exam session. My "cousin" and I got the tickets for the O2 Arena in London.
I think that "cousin" deserves a better explanation: we are not, in fact, cousins by blood. My mother and hers have been friends since childhood, so I've always had the habit of seeing him as part of the family.

We couldn't contain our excitement for that trip, so we bought the round-trip and hostel on the same day to ensure our presence there. But, you know, artists are unpredictable sometimes. It ended, a few months later, with a serious shoulder injury for the duo band's drummer and our broken dreams.

We claimed a refund for the concert ticket, but we couldn't have done the same for the plane and accommodation, so we opted for a semi-unknown Italian band concert in Camden Town on the same days.

Once again in London, we realized how far our accommodation was. That easily explained the cheap 18-pound price from Booking. The hostel was at the end of the Jubilee Line.
